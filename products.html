overview

Laser cutting is a precise and efficient manufacturing process used to cut, shape, or engrave materials using a focused, high-powered laser beam. This technology is widely utilized across various industries, including automotive, aerospace, electronics, jewelry, and signage.

How Laser Cutting Works

Laser Generation:

A laser beam is created using a gain medium (e.g., CO2, fiber, or crystal laser) and focused through lenses to a small, intense point.

Material Interaction:

The high energy of the laser melts, burns, vaporizes, or blows away the material, leaving a clean, precise cut.

Control System:

The process is controlled by a computer system (CNC or CAD/CAM software) to achieve intricate and repeatable designs.
