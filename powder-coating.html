Powder coating

Powder coating is a dry finishing process widely used to provide a protective and decorative finish to metal and non-metal surfaces. It is applied as a free-flowing, dry powder and then cured under heat to form a durable, uniform coating. This technique is popular due to its durability, environmental friendliness, and ability to produce a high-quality finish.

How Powder Coating Works

Preparation:

The surface is cleaned and pre-treated to remove dirt, oil, and rust to ensure proper adhesion of the powder.
Common cleaning methods include:
Sandblasting or grit blasting.
Chemical etching.
Degreasing and washing.

Application:

The powder is applied using an electrostatic spray gun, which charges the powder particles.
The charged powder particles adhere to the grounded object.
Application methods include:
Electrostatic Spray Coating (most common).
Fluidized Bed Coating (for thicker coatings or smaller objects).

Curing:

After the powder is applied, the coated object is placed in a curing oven.
Heat (typically 180°C to 200°C or 356°F to 392°F) melts the powder and causes it to bond, forming a smooth and durable layer.
